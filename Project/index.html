Our Diploma Courses

Computer Science

Computer Science (CS) is the study of computers, computational systems, algorithms, and software. It focuses on both the theory and practice of how computers work and how they can be used to solve real-world problems. A Computer Science course teaches students programming, problem-solving, data management, algorithms, hardware concepts, and emerging technologies like artificial intelligence and machine learning.

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering is one of the oldest and broadest branches of engineering. It deals with the design, development, manufacturing, operation, and maintenance of machines and mechanical systems. This course combines principles of physics, mathematics, material science, and engineering to design and analyze everything from small components to large systems like engines, vehicles, turbines, and industrial machines.

Automobile Engineering

Automobile Engineering is a specialized branch of engineering that focuses on the design, development, manufacturing, testing, and maintenance of automobiles — including cars, motorcycles, buses, trucks, and other motor vehicles. This course combines elements of mechanical, electrical, electronic, software, and safety engineering to create and improve automotive vehicles and their systems.

Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering is one of the oldest and broadest branches of engineering. It deals with the planning, design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment, such as buildings, roads, bridges, canals, dams, airports, and water supply systems. This course equips students with the knowledge to develop infrastructure that meets the needs of society while ensuring safety, sustainability, and environmental responsibility.

Information Technology

The Information Technology (IT) course provides students with the knowledge, skills, and hands-on experience needed to work with computer systems, software, networks, and data management. This course is designed to prepare individuals for a wide range of roles in the IT industry by focusing on both the theoretical foundations and practical applications of information technology.

Electrical Engineering

Electrical Engineering (EE) is a core branch of engineering that focuses on the study, design, and application of systems and equipment that use electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. It deals with power generation, transmission, distribution, electrical machines, control systems, and modern electronics. Electrical engineering plays a key role in building the infrastructure that powers industries, cities, homes, and future technologies like electric vehicles, renewable energy systems, smart grids, and robotics.

Electronics Engineering

Electronics Engineering is a branch of engineering that deals with the design, development, and application of electronic devices, circuits, and systems. It focuses on how electrons are controlled and manipulated to create modern technologies such as smartphones, computers, medical devices, communication systems, embedded systems, and much more. This course blends knowledge from physics, mathematics, computer science, and electrical engineering.

Pharmacy

Diploma in Pharmacy (D.Pharm) is a short-term professional course that trains students to work as qualified pharmacy technicians or assistants in hospitals, retail pharmacies, pharmaceutical companies, and healthcare settings. This program focuses on drug composition, manufacturing, dispensing, patient counseling, and pharmaceutical ethics. It provides both theoretical and practical knowledge needed to support pharmacists and ensure safe medication use.

Printing Technology

Printing Technology is a specialized branch of engineering and design that focuses on the science, processes, and machinery involved in producing printed materials. The course covers various aspects of traditional and modern printing methods, prepress operations, press management, post-press operations, and digital printing technologies. With the rapid advancement of digital media, printing technology now also includes 3D printing, packaging technology, and electronic publishing.
